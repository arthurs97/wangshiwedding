# Style Recommendations for Wedding Website

## Design Philosophy
**Goal**: Classy, elegant, timeless - not tacky or overdone. The design should feel sophisticated and warm, suitable for a wedding celebration.

---

## Color Palette Recommendations

### Background Colors (Cream/Off-White)
1. **#FAF8F3** (Warm Cream) - *Top Recommendation*
   - Warm, inviting, elegant
   - Slight warm undertone prevents it from feeling sterile
   - Excellent contrast with dark text

2. **#F7F5F0** (Soft Ivory)
   - Slightly more neutral than warm cream
   - Very sophisticated, works well in all lighting

3. **#FFFEF7** (Pure Cream)
   - Brightest option, very clean
   - Best for maximum readability

4. **#F5F3ED** (Muted Cream)
   - Most subtle, sophisticated
   - Slightly darker, more vintage feel

### Text Colors (Black/Dark Grey)
1. **#2C2C2C** (Charcoal) - *Top Recommendation*
   - Rich, readable, not harsh black
   - Professional and elegant
   - Better for extended reading than pure black

2. **#1A1A1A** (Near Black)
   - Maximum contrast option
   - Very readable, slightly more formal

3. **#333333** (Dark Grey)
   - Softer than charcoal
   - Good for body text if you want slightly less contrast

### Accent Colors (Optional - for highlights, links, etc.)
1. **#8B7355** (Muted Gold/Bronze) - *Top Recommendation*
   - Elegant, warm, complements cream backgrounds
   - Not too flashy, sophisticated

2. **#B8860B** (Dark Goldenrod)
   - More traditional gold
   - Works well but slightly more traditional

3. **#C9A961** (Soft Gold)
   - Lighter, more subtle
   - Very elegant

4. **#D4AF37** (Classic Gold)
   - Current default, works well
   - Slightly brighter than muted options

### Secondary Background (for sections, cards, etc.)
- **#FFFFFF** (Pure White) - for contrast sections
- **#F9F9F9** (Off-White) - subtle section separation

## Typography Recommendations

### Serif Fonts for Headers (Elegant, Classic)

1. **Cormorant Garamond** - *Top Recommendation*
   - Extremely elegant, refined serif
   - Excellent for wedding typography
   - Multiple weights available (300, 400, 600, 700)
   - Pairs beautifully with modern sans-serifs
   - Google Fonts: `Cormorant Garamond`

2. **Playfair Display** (Current)
   - Already in use, very elegant
   - Slightly more decorative
   - Great for large headings

3. **Crimson Text**
   - Classic, readable serif
   - More traditional book-like feel
   - Excellent for body text too if desired

4. **Lora**
   - Elegant, modern serif
   - Very readable, sophisticated
   - Good balance of classic and contemporary

5. **Merriweather**
   - Professional, clean serif
   - Excellent readability
   - Slightly more formal

### Sans-Serif Fonts for Body Text (Casual, Modern)

1. **Work Sans** - *Top Recommendation*
   - Modern, friendly, highly readable
   - Excellent for body text
   - Pairs beautifully with Cormorant Garamond
   - Multiple weights (300, 400, 500, 600)
   - Google Fonts: `Work Sans`

2. **Lato** (Current)
   - Already in use, very good choice
   - Friendly, professional
   - Excellent readability

3. **Open Sans**
   - Very popular, highly readable
   - Clean, modern, friendly
   - Slightly more neutral than Work Sans

4. **Source Sans Pro**
   - Professional, clean
   - Excellent for long-form reading
   - Slightly more formal

5. **Inter**
   - Modern, geometric
   - Excellent screen readability
   - Very contemporary feel

## Top Recommendation: Complete Style Package

### Colors
- **Background**: `#FAF8F3` (Warm Cream)
- **Text**: `#2C2C2C` (Charcoal)
- **Accent**: `#8B7355` (Muted Gold)
- **Secondary Background**: `#FFFFFF` (White for contrast)

### Typography
- **Headers**: `Cormorant Garamond` (weights: 400, 600, 700)
- **Body**: `Work Sans` (weights: 300, 400, 500)

### Rationale
This combination creates a sophisticated, warm, and elegant aesthetic:
- Cormorant Garamond provides classic elegance without being overly decorative
- Work Sans is modern and readable, creating a perfect contrast
- Warm cream background feels inviting and luxurious
- Charcoal text is readable without being harsh
- Muted gold accent adds warmth without being flashy

## Implementation Notes

1. **Font Weights**: Use 400 (regular) and 600 (semi-bold) for headers, 300 (light) and 400 (regular) for body text
2. **Line Height**: 1.6-1.8 for body text, 1.2-1.4 for headings
3. **Letter Spacing**: Slight increase (0.5-1px) for headers can add elegance
4. **Contrast**: Ensure WCAG AA compliance (4.5:1 for normal text, 3:1 for large text)
